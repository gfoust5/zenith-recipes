Protein Pancakes

Actually tastes like pancakes. Not a sad protein approximation of pancakes — real, fluffy, stackable pancakes with 35g of protein per serving.

440Calories
35gProtein
46gCarbs
10gFat
Prep 10 min Cook 15 min Servings 2 Macros shown per serving

Ingredients

  • 2 scoopsvanilla whey protein powder
  • 1 cupoat flour (blend rolled oats)
  • 2 largeeggs
  • 3/4 cupunsweetened almond milk
  • 1 tspbaking powder
  • 1 tspvanilla extract
  • 1 tbsphoney
  • Cooking sprayas needed
  • 1/2 cupfresh berries

Directions

  1. Whisk together protein powder, oat flour, and baking powder in a bowl.
  2. In a separate bowl, whisk eggs, almond milk, vanilla, and honey.
  3. Combine wet and dry ingredients. Stir until just mixed — small lumps are fine. Let batter rest 3 minutes.
  4. Heat a nonstick skillet over medium-low heat. Spray with cooking spray.
  5. Pour 1/4 cup batter per pancake. Cook until bubbles form and edges look set, about 2–3 minutes. Flip and cook 1–2 more minutes.
  6. Stack and top with fresh berries.

Coach's Note

Medium-low heat is the move here — protein powder burns faster than flour. If they're browning too fast, drop the heat. These reheat well in a toaster the next morning.
