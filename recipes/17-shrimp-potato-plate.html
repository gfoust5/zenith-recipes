✦ High heat is the move — sear fast, stay juicy · Baby potatoes need no peeling
What You Need
  • 450g large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 400g baby potatoes, halved
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp onion powder
  • 1/2 tsp cayenne pepper
  • 1/2 tsp dried oregano
  • Salt & pepper to taste
  • Cooking spray
  • Fresh parsley & lemon — optional
What You Need To Do
  1. 01

    Preheat oven to 425°F. Toss halved baby potatoes with olive oil, half the paprika, half the garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Spread on a baking sheet and roast 20 minutes, flipping once at 10 minutes.

  2. 02

    While potatoes roast, pat shrimp completely dry with paper towels. Season with remaining paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, cayenne, oregano, salt, and pepper.

  3. 03

    Heat a skillet over high heat and spray with cooking spray. Cook shrimp in a single layer 90 seconds per side until pink and slightly charred. Work in batches if needed — don't crowd the pan.

  4. 04

    Pull potatoes when golden and crispy. Plate potatoes, top with shrimp. Finish with parsley and a squeeze of lemon if using.

Coach's Note

Shrimp is one of the best protein-per-calorie foods on the planet — 38g protein for 390 calories is elite. The only thing that kills shrimp is wet shrimp, low heat, and a crowded pan. Pat them dry, get the pan screaming hot, and give them space. Baby potatoes are the lazy cook's best friend — no peeling, no cutting down, just halve and roast.
